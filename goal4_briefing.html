Goal Statement

Develop and operationalize a performance measurement framework that enables CROB to track outcomes, demonstrate impact, and make evidence-informed strategic decisions.

Key Objectives

  • Build a measurement framework with clear KPIs and benchmarks.
  • Track outreach, feedback, and outcomes in one place.
  • Use data to adjust outreach, staffing, and resources.
  • Build staff capacity for data entry, analysis, and visualization.

What Success Looks Like

Quarterly reports on time Standardized reports delivered each quarter.
Data quality scores Completeness, accuracy, and timeliness tracked consistently.
Data-informed decisions Strategy and resource allocation shaped by the data.
Stakeholder satisfaction Leaders trust the reporting format and transparency.

Measurement Priorities

  • Distressed areas reached.
  • People reached and topics discussed.
  • Quarterly and annual benchmarks with check-ins.
  • Annual total events and progress to goal.
  • Alignment with organizational reporting requirements.

Tracking Systems

  • Community feedback database.
  • Paper feedback forms at events and tablet/laptop capture.
  • Digital analytics feeding reporting dashboards.
  • Cross-reference event locations with fraud and risk corridors.
  • Track follow-up outcomes like complaints, FAIR Plan enrollments, and helpline calls.

Data-Driven Adjustments

  • Monthly check-ins on data completeness.
  • Quarterly data review meetings and cleanup.
  • Live dashboard showing current completeness.
  • Actionable heat maps linked to distressed ZIPs, uninsured motorists, and senior concentration.
  • Leadership recommendations based on demographic insights.

Internal Capacity

  • Train staff on entry standards and quality control.
  • Make timely Quorum entry a performance KPI.
  • Define what “on time” means.
  • Identify barriers that slow complete entry.
  • Keep discussing easier, more consistent data entry methods.

Must Be Done Now

Data cleanup Remove all material fields from filtering in the heat map and Quorum.
Timely entry KPI Define “on time” and include it in annual reviews.
Quarterly cleanup Ensure all fields are filled out and usable for reporting.
Calendar → Quorum automation Explore automation as a near-future goal with IT approval.

The Case for Using AI

  • AI is currently blocked on work computers; IT approval is required.
  • Useful for heat maps, coverage visuals, and cross-referencing need vs. outreach.
  • Only useful if the source data is consistent and cleaned first.

Community Feedback

  • Send surveys every 6 months for chambers and consider internal surveys.
  • Paper forms or pre-loaded tablets reduce QR-code barriers for seniors.
  • Staff should ask directly and personally for feedback.
  • Digitize paper forms when needed.

Retreat Discussion: Quorum & Data Entry

Why it is hard Entry feels like extra admin work, deadlines are unclear, and staff do not always see the impact.
What to discuss What would make on-time entry easier, and what decisions would improve with better data?
Open questions What fields are truly necessary, and how do we make every entry more actionable?
